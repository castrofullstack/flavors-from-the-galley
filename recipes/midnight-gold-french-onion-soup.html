Midnight Gold French Onion Soup

A deep, jammy French onion soup built with harvested beef tallow, 24-hour bone broth, toasted baguette, and a bubbling Swiss-Gruyere crown.

Prep time 15 minutes Cook time 1 hour Yields 4 servings

The philosophy

Patience Turns Onions Into Gold

A great French onion soup is an exercise in patience. The harvested tallow caramelizes the onions into a deep mahogany base, then your 24-hour bone broth brings the clean, rich backbone that makes the whole bowl feel slow-built and intentional.

Ingredients

What Goes In

The Base

  • 2 quarts Liquid Gold beef bone broth

The Fat

  • 2 tbsp harvested beef tallow, or butter

The Onions

  • 4-5 large yellow onions, thinly sliced

The Bread

  • 1 Trader Joe's Artisan Style Baguette, sliced into 1-inch thick rounds

The Cheese

  • 1 1/2 cups Trader Joe's Shredded Swiss & Gruyere Blend

The Aromatics

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf

The Deglaze

  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or sherry, optional for additional depth

The Seasoning

  • To taste salt and black pepper, remembering that the broth is already seasoned

Preparation

Low, Slow, Broiled

  1. The caramelization: Melt the beef tallow in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium-low heat. Add the sliced onions with a pinch of salt and cook slowly, stirring occasionally, for 35-45 minutes. You are looking for a deep mahogany onion jam, not merely browned onions. If the onions begin sticking, add a small splash of water to release the flavor and keep caramelizing.
  2. The aromatics: Add the minced garlic and fresh thyme. Saute for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. If using wine or sherry, pour it into the pot now to deglaze the pan, scraping up the browned fond from the bottom.
  3. The simmer: Pour in the beef bone broth and add the bay leaf. Bring the soup to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and simmer uncovered for 20 minutes so the sweetness of the onions and richness of the broth fully meld.
  4. The artisan crouton: While the soup simmers, arrange the baguette slices on a baking sheet. Toast in your COSORI oven at 400°F (200°C) until golden and crisp on both sides.
  5. The Gruyere crown: Ladle the hot soup into oven-safe bowls. Top each bowl with 1-2 toasted bread rounds and a generous handful of the Swiss & Gruyere blend.
  6. The final broil: Place the bowls back into the COSORI oven using the Broil setting for 3-5 minutes, until the cheese is melted, bubbling, and beautifully browned across the surface.

Galley tip

Cheesy Rafts, Cleaner Finish

  • If you do not have oven-safe crocks, melt the cheese directly onto the toasted bread in the air fryer first, then float the cheesy rafts on top of the soup immediately before serving.
  • This keeps the bread crunchier and prevents difficult baked-on cheese cleanup.
  • Serve piping hot with additional cracked black pepper and a small side salad or roasted vegetables.
  • The soup becomes even deeper in flavor the next day, making it ideal for meal prep or cold-weather comfort food.
