Midnight Gold Shepherd's Pie

Grass-fed beef, Guinness deglaze, Midnight Gold bone broth, roasted garlic, and garlic confit under a Yukon Gold and sweet potato mash.

Ready in 1 hour 20 minutes Serves 8 Bake 400°F

The galley take

Comfort Food With Backbone

This is comfort food with backbone. The filling gets its depth from grass-fed beef, Midnight Gold bone broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire, garlic confit, and a modest Guinness deglaze. The topping leans into Portuguese-Irish instincts: earthy Yukon Golds for structure, sweet potatoes for warmth, and roasted garlic for mellow sweetness.

Ingredients

What Goes In

For the Filling

  • 2 lbs BJ's grass-fed ground beef
  • 1 tbsp garlic confit oil
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and diced small
  • 4-6 cloves garlic confit, mashed into a paste
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp dried rosemary, crushed between your fingers
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup Guinness
  • 1 1/2 cups Midnight Gold bone broth
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups frozen peas
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard or balsamic vinegar, optional
  • To taste kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the Topping

  • 1 1/2 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 1/2 lbs sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 head roasted garlic, cloves squeezed out
  • 5 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream or whole milk, warmed
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Pinch freshly grated nutmeg, optional
  • To taste kosher salt

Optional Finish

  • 1 tbsp melted butter for brushing the ridges
  • 2-4 minutes under the broiler for extra color

Method

Layer, Bake, Rest

  1. Preheat and prep: Preheat the oven to 400°F. Have a 9 x 13-inch baking dish ready.
  2. Build the mash: Place the Yukon Golds and sweet potatoes in a large pot of cold salted water. Bring to a boil and cook until fork-tender, 15 to 20 minutes. Drain thoroughly and let the potatoes sit in the warm pot for 1 to 2 minutes so excess steam escapes. Mash with the roasted garlic, butter, warm cream, egg yolk, and optional nutmeg until smooth and cohesive. Season to taste with salt.
  3. Brown the beef the right way: Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the garlic confit oil, then add the beef and brown it until well colored. If your pan is crowded, work in batches so the meat sears instead of steams. Season lightly with salt and black pepper. Spoon off excess fat if needed, leaving about 1 tablespoon in the pan.
  4. Build the base: Add the onion and carrots and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, until softened and beginning to take on color. Stir in the garlic confit paste, tomato paste, thyme, and rosemary. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring frequently, until the tomato paste darkens slightly and smells sweet, savory, and concentrated.
  5. Deglaze with Guinness: Sprinkle the flour over the mixture and stir for 1 minute to coat. Pour in the Guinness and scrape the pan well to release the fond. Let it simmer for 3 to 5 minutes, until reduced by about half and the raw beer edge is gone.
  6. Finish the gravy: Pour in the Midnight Gold bone broth and stir in the Worcestershire sauce. Simmer for 4 to 6 minutes, until the mixture turns glossy and thick but still slightly loose. Fold in the frozen peas and cook just 1 to 2 minutes more. Taste and adjust salt and pepper.
  7. Assemble: Transfer the filling to the baking dish. Spread the mash over the top and use a fork to create ridges all over the surface. Brush lightly with melted butter if using.
  8. Bake: Bake for 28 to 32 minutes, until bubbling at the edges and hot throughout. For extra color, broil for 2 to 4 minutes at the end, watching closely, until the ridges are deeply golden and lightly caramelized.
  9. Rest and serve: Let the pie rest for 10 to 15 minutes before serving. That short rest helps the filling settle and gives you cleaner, more satisfying portions.

Galley notes

Quality Assured

  • Keep the Guinness to 1/3 cup. It should deepen the filling, not dominate it.
  • If your bone broth is cold and gelatinous from the fridge, that is exactly what you want. It will melt into the filling and create a beautiful gravy.
  • Sweet potatoes brown faster than regular potatoes, so watch the pie closely during the final minutes and especially under the broiler.
  • To split the work over two days, make the broth, roasted garlic, and garlic confit on Saturday, then assemble and bake the pie on Sunday.
  • Technically, beef makes this closer to a cottage pie, but the spirit here is all comfort-food tradition. Call it what makes you happy and serve it hot.
